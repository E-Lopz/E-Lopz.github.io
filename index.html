Some of my projects

Downscalling of climate models

In collaboration with a dedicated team of five, we embarked on a five-month project exploring climate model downscaling. During this endeavor, we sought guidance from researchers at MILA. Integrating geological and topological data into the downscaling process, we utilized UNets and successfully adapted ERA5 data to enhance our dataset. By integrating geological and topological data into climate model downscaling, we aimed to enhance the precision and reliability of our models. The incorporation of UNets and the adaptation of ERA5 data further contributed to refining our dataset. The importance of this lies in the potential to better understand and predict localized climate patterns, aiding in more accurate climate projections and informed decision-making. This project underscores the practical applications of downscaling methodologies in contributing to the broader understanding of climate science and its implications for our world.

Sentiment Analysis

The inherent complexity of natural language processing has spurred innovation in data preprocessing and textual data modeling techniques. Two primary approaches to text classification have emerged: rule-based approaches and machine learning-based approaches. Machine learning techniques such as logistic regression, recurrent neural networks, and deep neural networks have demonstrated strong performances in various text classification problems. This study compares the performances of different machine learning and deep learning models for text classification. Evaluated models include logistic regression, multilayer perceptron, convolutional-recurrent neural networks (CNN-RNN), and an ensemble method. The focus is on exploring various modeling techniques and using ensemble methods to enhance the performance and robustness of text sentiment classification models. In summary, the project aims to explore NLP text processing methods, apply them, and utilize concepts learned in the course with multiple machine (and deep) learning models, ultimately comparing the results obtained.

Country Data Mining

In this project, we're tasked with collecting and cleaning both numerical and categorical data related to countries worldwide. The data, sourced from several tables on the internet, undergoes a thorough cleaning process, including the discretization of all features (columns). We employ data mining techniques to uncover patterns, ensure data accuracy through cleansing, and use visualization methods to present a clear picture of global data. Additionally, strategic feature selection is applied to focus on the most relevant variables. The goal is to provide a comprehensive and well-processed dataset for in-depth analysis and insights into global country statistics.
